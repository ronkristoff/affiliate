Direction 6 — Affiliate Portal
Mobile-first · White-label
Direction 1 — Command Center
A bold 240px blue sidebar inspired by Linear — our established design language in full execution. The sidebar is the product's identity: brand-primary blue from header to footer, icon+label nav items, with the content area as a clean white workspace. Highest visual clarity between navigation and content surfaces.
Linear-inspired Bold sidebar Desktop-primary Brand-forward Established pattern

Strengths

  • Brand color immediately visible
  • Navigation always in context
  • Alex feels "in command"
  • Familiar SaaS pattern
  • Status badges pop on white

Tradeoffs

  • Sidebar takes 240px of real estate
  • Blue sidebar can feel heavy
  • Less modern than icon-only nav

Direction 2 — Editorial Dark
A dark-mode-first interface with icon-only sidebar and activity-timeline-centered layout. Data feels like a live feed — financial events as a narrative stream. Inspired by GitHub's developer-grade aesthetic. Strongest for users who live in the tool all day and prefer low visual fatigue.
Dark mode Icon sidebar Timeline-first Developer feel High contrast

Strengths

  • Maximizes content area
  • Low visual fatigue for daily use
  • Timeline = natural for audit trail
  • High contrast status badges

Tradeoffs

  • Departs from light-mode brief
  • Less welcoming to non-technical users
  • Affiliate portal needs separate treatment
Direction 3 — Clean Minimal
Horizontal top navigation eliminates the sidebar entirely, maximizing the content canvas. Light, airy, and approachable — feels like a modern analytics tool. The "Monday morning confidence" check is served by a 4-KPI strip and two action-cards below the fold. Best for users who value breathing room over navigation density.
Horizontal nav Maximum whitespace Analytics feel Approachable Wide content area

Strengths

  • Most content space of any direction
  • Lower visual density feels friendly
  • Progress bars add visual delight
  • Actionable cards are immediately clear

Tradeoffs

  • Horizontal nav can get crowded at 6+ items
  • Less "command center" feel
  • Weaker brand presence
Direction 4 — Trust & Warmth
A white sidebar with colored accent nav-items, a gradient hero card at the top of the content, and a person-centric activity feed using avatar initials. Treats every affiliate as a human — names and amounts together, not anonymous rows. Highest emotional resonance for the "I care about my affiliates" SaaS Owner persona. Inspired by Notion's warmth.
Human-first White sidebar Gradient hero Avatar feed Notion-inspired

Strengths

  • Highest emotional warmth
  • Affiliates feel like people, not rows
  • Gradient hero creates visual anchor
  • Approachable for non-technical Alexes

Tradeoffs

  • Purple departs from brand tokens
  • Activity feed hides data density
  • Less "financial command" feel
Direction 5 — Data-Dense Pro
Maximum information density with a 5-KPI strip, tabbed navigation within the content area, and a full-bleed data table as the primary UI surface. Designed for Alex's power-user mode: managing 200+ affiliates and running weekly data reviews. Every pixel serves data. Inspired by Stripe's dashboard density with a dark background for contrast.
Power user Maximum density Tabbed content Table-first Stripe-inspired

Strengths

  • Most data visible at once
  • 5-KPI row answers weekly questions fast
  • Tabbed views keep context
  • Satisfying for data-driven Alexes

Tradeoffs

  • Overwhelming for new users
  • Dark theme adds implementation cost
  • Less friendly for occasional users
Direction 6 — Affiliate Portal (Mobile-First)
This direction focuses on Jamie's surface — the white-labeled affiliate portal on mobile. Two phones side by side demonstrate the white-label contract: the same component tree rendering in completely different brand colors (Alex's blue vs. a tenant's orange). Jamie's "Commission Confirmed" screenshot moment is the design centerpiece. This is the portal Jamie opens from her phone between tasks.
Affiliate portal Mobile-first White-label demo Jamie's surface Screenshot moment

Strengths

  • White-label proven in the mockup
  • Earnings hero is immediately satisfying
  • One-tap copy link placement is obvious
  • Commission status badge is screenshot-worthy

Tradeoffs

  • Only shows affiliate surface
  • Needs pairing with an Owner dashboard direction
Evaluation Guide
What to look for as you explore

🧭
Layout Intuitiveness

Which information hierarchy matches Alex's Monday morning check-in priority? Does the overview answer "how much did affiliates drive, any commissions to approve, any payouts to run?" above the fold?

⚡
Interaction Style

Which navigation pattern lets Alex move from Overview → Commissions → Payouts in the fewest clicks? Which feels like it anticipates his workflow rather than requiring him to learn it?

⚖️
Visual Weight

Which visual density — from airy minimal (D3) to dense pro (D5) — feels right for a financial product that needs to project precision and accuracy? Does it feel trustworthy or overwhelming?

🎨
Brand Alignment

Recall the brand tokens: Brand Primary #10409a, surface white #fff, page background #f2f2f2. Which direction makes those tokens feel intentional, not incidental? Which one is unmistakably salig-affiliate?

💼
Role Scoping Readiness

Dana (Manager) shouldn't see a Payouts tab. Which navigation pattern makes it easiest to simply remove tabs per role — without locked icons, without grey-outs, without explaining absence?

🤝
Emotional Resonance

Alex needs to feel "in command." Which direction triggers that feeling most immediately? Which would make him think "I built something real here" when he sees that ₱84,200 affiliate MRR number?
